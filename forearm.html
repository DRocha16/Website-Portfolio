Forearm Prothesis w/ Osseointegration

Phase 1: Osseointegration


This is a project being made for a course of my masters.

In this first phase, I'm developing an implant to fix the prothesis to the bone (osseointegration).

Why osseointegration? Because it's a way to improve the feedback that the amputee has from the prothesis movement. Every touch on the prothesis will produce vibrations in it that will be carried to the bone, where this can be naturally sensed by the body. As opposite to what would happen in the non invasive prothesis, where amputees feel that they have something stitched to their arm, instead of feeling that the prothesis is an integral part of their body.

The implant mode of operation is based on a porous fixture that is permatently implanted into the bone, it is porous to let the bone grow through it, integrating with it.

Then, an abutment will be screwed to the fixture. On the other hand, the abutment will fit into a 2 lock mechanism conector. The first mechanism is meant for a quick first fit (it is partly inspired in the water hoses quick fit mechanism), then for added safety there's a screw lock mechanism.
